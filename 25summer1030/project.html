Course Description

This course is a self-contained introduction to Matrix Theory and Linear Algebra. Topics include: systems of linear equations, vectors in R^n, matrices, spans, linear independence, bases, dimension, linear transformations in R^n, determinants, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalization, applications.
Prerequisites:Nova Scotia Advanced Mathematics 11 or 12 (or equivalent).

Approximate content of lectures

  1. May 05: Check-in & syllabus overview. Linear systems. Sections 1.1-1.2.
  2. May 07: Solving linear systems via row reduction. Echelon form. Sections 1.3-1.4.
  3. May 09: Gauss-Jordan elimination. Reduced row echelon form. Sections 1.4-1.5.
  4. May 12: Review. Homogeneous systems. Sections 1.6-1.8.
  5. May 14: Points & vectors. Length of a vector. Sections 2.1-2.4.
  6. May 16: asynchronous activity. (TBA)
  7. May 19: Dot and cross products. Sections 2.5-2.6
  8. May 21: Matrix arithmetic. Sections 4.1-4.4.
  9. May 23: Linear systems & matrices. Inverse matrices. Section 4.5.
  10. May 26: Spans & linear independence. Sections 5.1-5.2
  11. May 28: Subspaces of R^n. Basis and dimension. Sections 5.3-5.4.
  12. May 29: Midterm test.
  13. May 30: Column space and null space. Section 5.5.
  14. Jun 02: Linear transformations. Section 6.1-6.2.
  15. Jun 04: More on linear transformations. Sections 6.3-6.4.
  16. Jun 06: Determinants. Sections 7.1-7.5
  17. Jun 09: Eigenvectors and eigenalues. Sections 8.1-8.3
  18. Jun 11: Diagonalization. Section 8.4.
  19. Jun 13: Applications. Sections 8.5-8.7.
  20. Jun 16: catch-up or review class.
  21. Jun 18: review class.
  22. Jun 20: Final exam.
